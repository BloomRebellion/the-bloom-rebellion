About Us

Our Story

Manifesto

We are the mothers who whispered instead of spoke.
The women who hid the jar in the cabinet, the conversation at the dinner table, the relief on our own faces.

We were told it made us unfit. Reckless. Less than. So we stayed quiet — and the silence became its own kind of erasure.

But we were never absent from this plant's history. We were its midwives, its healers, its first scientists, long before the world had a word for what we were doing.

The Bloom Rebellion exists because silence was never going to be the answer. Evidence is. Community is. Telling the truth, out loud, together — that's the rebellion.

We are rooted in science. Grounded in love. Growing in truth.

Meet the Founder

Some movements begin with a business plan. This one began with questions.

Questions about why women were still being judged for choosing a plant. Questions about why mothers were forced into silence instead of supported with science. Questions about why so many of the women who built the cannabis movement had been overlooked, erased, or forgotten.

The Bloom Rebellion was born from the belief that education can replace fear, history can challenge stigma, and community can change lives. This isn't just a website — it's a growing archive of women's voices, scientific research, lived experiences, and the stories that deserve to be remembered.

My Story

I'm a wife, a mother, and a professional working in the legal cannabis industry. Like many women, I've seen firsthand how quickly conversations about cannabis can become conversations about shame, especially when mothers are involved.

I've watched misinformation spread faster than evidence. I've seen women dismissed instead of heard. I've seen history rewritten until the women who helped build this movement were barely mentioned.

I created The Bloom Rebellion because I wanted something different. A place where curiosity is welcomed. Where difficult conversations can happen respectfully. Where science comes before stigma. Where women don't have to choose between honesty and acceptance.

Why "The Bloom Rebellion"?

Because blooming is an act of courage. Plants bloom despite difficult conditions. Women do too.

The rebellion isn't about anger. It's about refusing to let misinformation define our stories any longer. It's about reclaiming history, sharing evidence, and supporting one another — and creating a future where no woman feels isolated for asking questions about cannabis, health, motherhood, or advocacy.
