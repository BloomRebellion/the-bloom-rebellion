🌿 The Evidence

Science Over Stigma. Evidence Over Opinion.

The Bloom Rebellion Evidence Library exists to translate peer-reviewed cannabis research into clear, readable language. Every article in this section is based on published scientific studies, systematic reviews, or clinical research.

We do not make claims without sources. We do not exaggerate findings. We present the science as it is — complete with strengths, limitations, and unanswered questions. Cannabis research is still evolving. This library evolves with it.

How to Read This Library

Every article breaks down one study, the same way

  • Research Question — what scientists were trying to understand
  • Study Design — how the research was conducted
  • Key Findings — what the study actually found
  • Limitations — what the study cannot prove
  • Why It Matters — what it means in real life
  • Citation — the original peer-reviewed source

Each study is also labeled with an evidence level so readers can understand its strength at a glance.

🧪 Evidence Levels

How strong is the science?

Each study is tagged so readers can understand strength at a glance.

  • Systematic Review / Meta-analysis (strongest evidence)
  • Randomized Controlled Trial
  • Cohort or observational study
  • Small clinical trial
  • Case studies or case series
  • Laboratory / preclinical research
  • Historical or foundational discovery

Closing Statement

Cannabis science is still developing. Some areas are well-studied. Others are still emerging. Many questions remain unanswered.

The goal of this library is not to promote certainty where none exists — but to make the evidence accessible, transparent, and understandable to everyone who wants to learn it.
